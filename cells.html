Cells

Cells are the basic unit of life. There are two types of cells: Eukaryotic and Prokaryotic cells.

Eukaryotic Cells

Simplified Explanation

A eukaryote is a cell or organism that possesses a clearly defined nucleus. The cells of all multicellular organisms (plants, animals, and fungi) are eukaryotic. Algae and protists also are eukaryotic organisms.

Parts of the Eukaryotic cells

  1. mitochondria
  2. chloroplasts
  3. the endoplasmic reticulum
  4. the Golgi apparatus
  5. lysosomes

Prokaryotic

Simplified Explanation

Prokaryotic cells are single-celled microorganisms known to be the earliest on earth. Prokaryotes include Bacteria and Archaea. The photosynthetic prokaryotes include cyanobacteria that perform photosynthesis. A prokaryotic cell consists of a single membrane and therefore, all the reactions occur within the cytoplasm. They can be free-living or parasites.

Parts of the Prokaryotic Cells

  1. Cell wall
  2. Cell membrane
  3. Capsule
  4. Pili
  5. Flagella
  6. Ribosomes
  7. Plasmids
